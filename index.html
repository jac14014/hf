Core Mandate

Building Governance Capacity
Before Thresholds Are Crossed

Founded on the principle that effective governance must anticipate — not merely react to — systemic transitions, the Foundation operates at the intersection of legal architecture, ecological science, and institutional design.

⚖

Legal Architecture

Designing legal instruments and governance frameworks adapted to the pre-threshold condition — before irreversible transitions occur.

🌿

Ecological Field Research

🎓

Fellowship Program

🤝

Institutional Partnerships

Building coalitions across academic, governmental, and civil society institutions to operationalise the c-ECO framework at scale.

Amazon Living Lab

Field Science at the Frontier

The Amazon Living Lab is the Foundation's primary field research platform, positioned at a biome-scale threshold zone in the Brazilian Amazon. It provides real-world data on governance failures, ecological tipping points, and institutional responses.

The Lab generates empirical inputs for the c-ECO Framework and the Threshold Function Protocol, operating as a bridge between theoretical governance models and on-the-ground realities.
