Field Research Platform

Amazon Living Lab

A biome-scale field research platform in the Brazilian Amazon, generating empirical data on threshold governance at the frontier of ecological change.

About the Lab

Governance at the
Ecological Frontier

The Amazon Living Lab positions the Foundation's research within one of the world's most significant ecological threshold zones — the Brazilian Amazon, a biome under sustained pressure from deforestation, climate change, and governance failure.

The Lab is not a conventional research station. It is a living system for testing governance frameworks against the complex realities of frontier ecology: contested land rights, institutional fragmentation, indigenous governance, and rapid ecological transition.

Its outputs feed directly into the c-ECO Framework and the Threshold Function Protocol, providing the empirical grounding that distinguishes these frameworks from purely theoretical models.

Research Axes

What the Lab Studies

🌱

Ecological Threshold Monitoring

Tracking indicators of ecological threshold proximity — deforestation rates, biodiversity signals, hydrological shifts — to establish empirical baselines for governance activation thresholds.

⚖

Legal & Institutional Mapping

Mapping the legal and institutional landscape of the Amazon frontier: land tenure, environmental law, indigenous rights, and governance authority — identifying gaps and failure points.

🏛

Governance Instrument Testing

Field-testing components of the c-ECO Framework and TFP in live governance contexts, evaluating their operational effectiveness and institutional feasibility.

🤝

Indigenous Governance Integration

Documenting and integrating indigenous governance traditions into the anticipatory governance framework — recognising traditional ecological knowledge as a core input to threshold science.

Outputs

What the Lab Produces

The Lab generates outputs across three domains, each feeding back into the Foundation's programme portfolio:

  • Empirical datasets on ecological threshold indicators
  • Governance diagnostic reports on frontier institutional systems
  • Field-validated governance instrument specifications
  • Case studies for the c-ECO Framework and TFP
  • Policy briefs for governmental and international audiences

All Lab outputs are developed in collaboration with the c-eco.io research platform, which manages technical documentation, modelling, and publication of results.
