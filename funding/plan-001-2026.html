C-ECO Amazon Lab Funding Plan

Corrected Production Version · June 2026
The Hasse Foundation’s strongest funding position is not as another conservation applicant. It is the governance, traceability, and threshold-analysis partner that helps conservation, research, and resilience projects prove why action is needed before collapse — and how funders can verify that action occurred.

Audit Note. This plan preserves the core strategy but corrects eligibility, timing, and probability assumptions. Hasse should avoid presenting itself as a local implementation NGO and instead position itself as a U.S. 501(c)(3) technical, scientific, governance, and verification partner.

External Language

“The Hasse Foundation supports Brazilian-led environmental and resilience initiatives by contributing systemic governance methodology, scientific verification, risk-analysis frameworks, traceability-driven reporting, and international research capacity.”

C-ECO / Amazon Lab Value Proposition
  • What risk is visible before institutional failure occurs?
  • Which ecological, infrastructure, or social thresholds are approaching irreversibility?
  • Which governance actors have authority to act before the threshold is crossed?
  • How can monitoring data be translated into accountable decisions?
  • How can funders verify measurable, auditable outcomes?

TFP — Threshold Function Protocol: maps systemic position, velocity, uncertainty, and reversibility liquidity to identify movement from manageable risk toward irreversible failure.

TDR — Traceability-Driven Reporting: links funding, activities, outputs, outcomes, and governance decisions into an auditable chain.
