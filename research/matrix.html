Functional Evidence Matrix (FEM)

Systematic Mapping, Functional Coding, and Comparative Analysis of Operational Governance Frameworks for Threshold Dynamics, Systemic Risk, and Irreversibility

The Functional Evidence Matrix (FEM) is the primary analytical dataset supporting the c-ECO Systematic Mapping Study. Rather than organizing the literature by discipline or application domain alone, the FEM codes each framework according to seven operational governance functions derived from the literature itself. This enables comparison across theories, methods, standards, policy frameworks, and operational systems while preserving methodological transparency and reproducibility.

This is not yet a formal gap analysis. This page operationalizes the screening stage of the review protocol. Scores remain provisional and require full-text verification, DOI validation, source-status confirmation, and verbatim limitation extraction.

Protocol freeze: The review protocol is frozen as of Version 1.3. Subsequent changes are restricted to data corrections, source verification, formula correction, and corpus expansion.
